# RERA Practice Exam 1

## Exam Instructions

- **Time Limit:** 60 minutes
- **Questions:** 40 multiple choice
- **Passing Score:** 75% (30/40 correct)
- **Format:** Select the single best answer for each question

Read each question carefully. Manage your time—spend no more than 90 seconds per question.

---

## Question 1

What is the primary regulatory body responsible for licensing real estate professionals in Dubai?

- [ ] Dubai Land Department (DLD)
- [x] Real Estate Regulatory Agency (RERA)
- [ ] Dubai Economic Department
- [ ] Ministry of Economy

**Explanation:** RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Agency) is the regulatory arm of DLD responsible for licensing and regulating real estate professionals, including brokers, agents, and developers in Dubai.

---

## Question 2

A broker receives a cash payment of AED 50,000 from a client for a property transaction. According to RERA regulations, what should the broker do?

- [ ] Accept the payment and issue a personal receipt
- [ ] Refuse the payment entirely
- [x] Deposit the funds into the company's escrow account and issue an official receipt
- [ ] Keep the funds until the transaction is completed

**Explanation:** All client funds must be deposited into the brokerage's designated escrow account. Brokers must never accept cash personally and must always issue official company receipts for all transactions.

---

## Question 3

What is the standard Dubai Land Department registration fee for property transactions?

- [ ] 2% of property value
- [x] 4% of property value
- [ ] 5% of property value
- [ ] 6% of property value

**Explanation:** The DLD charges a 4% registration fee on all property transactions in Dubai, which is typically split between buyer and seller (2% each) unless negotiated otherwise.

---

## Question 4

Under RERA's Code of Ethics, which of the following is considered a conflict of interest?

- [ ] Representing the seller in a transaction
- [ ] Working with multiple buyers on different properties
- [x] Representing both buyer and seller in the same transaction without disclosure
- [ ] Referring a client to a mortgage broker

**Explanation:** Dual agency (representing both parties) without full written disclosure and consent from both parties is a conflict of interest. Agents must disclose all material relationships that could affect their objectivity.

---

## Question 5

What is the maximum security deposit a landlord can legally collect for an unfurnished residential property in Dubai?

- [ ] One month's rent
- [ ] Three months' rent
- [x] 5% of annual rent
- [ ] 10% of annual rent

**Explanation:** For unfurnished properties, landlords can collect a maximum security deposit of 5% of the annual rent. For furnished properties, the maximum is 10% of annual rent.

---

## Question 6

A tenant wishes to terminate their tenancy contract early. According to Dubai tenancy law, what notice period must they provide?

- [ ] 30 days
- [ ] 60 days
- [x] 90 days (unless otherwise specified in the contract)
- [ ] 180 days

**Explanation:** The standard notice period for early termination is 90 days, unless a different period is specified in the tenancy contract. The tenant may also be liable for early termination penalties as specified in the contract.

---

## Question 7

What document must a developer obtain before advertising and selling off-plan properties in Dubai?

- [ ] Building permit
- [ ] Commercial license
- [x] RERA project registration and escrow account
- [ ] Municipality approval only

**Explanation:** Developers must register the project with RERA and establish an escrow account before marketing or selling any off-plan units. This protects buyers by ensuring funds are used only for the designated project.

---

## Question 8

An Oqood certificate is:

- [ ] A final title deed for completed property
- [x] A preliminary registration document for off-plan property purchases
- [ ] A no-objection certificate from the developer
- [ ] A mortgage approval document

**Explanation:** Oqood is the interim registration system for off-plan properties. It records the buyer's interest in the property before completion, when a full title deed will be issued.

---

## Question 9

According to RERA regulations, within how many days must a real estate agent inform the client of any material change affecting the property?

- [ ] Immediately
- [x] Within 3 business days
- [ ] Within 7 business days
- [ ] Within 14 business days

**Explanation:** Agents must disclose any material changes or information that could affect the client's decision within 3 business days of becoming aware of such information.

---

## Question 10

What is the primary purpose of a Form F (MOU) in Dubai real estate transactions?

- [ ] To register the property with DLD
- [ ] To apply for a mortgage
- [x] To document the agreed terms between buyer and seller before transfer
- [ ] To cancel a tenancy contract

**Explanation:** Form F (Memorandum of Understanding) is the standard contract used to document the agreed terms between buyer and seller, including price, deposit, and conditions, before the property transfer is completed at DLD.

---

## Question 11

When calculating gross rental yield, which formula is correct?

- [x] (Annual Rent / Purchase Price) × 100
- [ ] (Monthly Rent / Purchase Price) × 100
- [ ] (Annual Rent - Expenses) / Purchase Price × 100
- [ ] (Purchase Price / Annual Rent) × 100

**Explanation:** Gross rental yield is calculated by dividing the annual rental income by the purchase price and multiplying by 100 to get a percentage. Net yield subtracts expenses from the annual rent.

---

## Question 12

A property is purchased for AED 1,000,000 and generates annual rent of AED 70,000. What is the gross rental yield?

- [ ] 5%
- [ ] 6%
- [x] 7%
- [ ] 8%

**Explanation:** Gross yield = (AED 70,000 / AED 1,000,000) × 100 = 7%. This represents the annual rental return as a percentage of the purchase price before expenses.

---

## Question 13

Under the Dubai Landlord-Tenant Law, a landlord can evict a tenant for personal use of the property by providing:

- [ ] 30 days notice
- [ ] 6 months notice
- [x] 12 months notarized notice
- [ ] Immediate eviction rights

**Explanation:** For personal use eviction, the landlord must provide 12 months written notice through notary public or registered mail. The landlord must also prove they don't own another suitable property.

---

## Question 14

Which of the following is NOT a valid reason for landlord to seek eviction under Dubai tenancy law?

- [ ] Non-payment of rent
- [ ] Property required for major renovation
- [ ] Personal use by landlord
- [x] To increase rent beyond RERA index

**Explanation:** Seeking higher rent is not a valid eviction ground. Valid reasons include non-payment, illegal use, damage to property, personal use, sale, or major renovation requiring vacant possession.

---

## Question 15

What percentage of construction must be completed before a developer can release escrow funds for off-plan projects?

- [ ] 10%
- [x] 20%
- [ ] 50%
- [ ] 80%

**Explanation:** Developers can only access escrow funds according to construction milestones, with the first release typically at 20% completion. This protects buyers by ensuring funds are used for actual construction progress.

---

## Question 16

A broker's license renewal must be completed within how many days of expiration?

- [ ] 14 days
- [x] 30 days
- [ ] 60 days
- [ ] 90 days

**Explanation:** Brokers must renew their license within 30 days of expiration. Operating with an expired license is a violation that can result in fines and suspension of trading activities.

---

## Question 17

The RERA Rental Index is used to:

- [ ] Set minimum rental prices
- [x] Determine permissible annual rent increases
- [ ] Calculate property taxes
- [ ] Establish broker commissions

**Explanation:** The RERA Rental Index provides benchmarks for different areas and property types. Rent increases are capped based on how far below market rate the current rent is, with maximum increases of 20% for rents significantly below market.

---

## Question 18

Which of the following best describes a broker's fiduciary duty?

- [ ] Maximizing commission on all transactions
- [ ] Following client instructions without question
- [x] Acting in the client's best interest while maintaining honesty with all parties
- [ ] Completing transactions as quickly as possible

**Explanation:** Fiduciary duty requires brokers to prioritize their client's interests, maintain confidentiality, provide full disclosure, and exercise reasonable care and skill—while remaining honest with all parties involved.

---

## Question 19

An agent discovers that a property they are marketing has significant structural damage. According to RERA ethics, the agent must:

- [ ] Wait for the buyer to ask about property condition
- [ ] Only disclose if the damage is visible
- [x] Immediately disclose the defect to all potential buyers
- [ ] Recommend the buyer hire an inspector

**Explanation:** Material defects must be disclosed immediately to all potential buyers. Failure to disclose known defects is an ethical violation that can result in license suspension and legal liability.

---

## Question 20

To convert square feet to square meters, you multiply by:

- [ ] 0.0645
- [x] 0.0929
- [ ] 10.764
- [ ] 3.281

**Explanation:** 1 square foot = 0.0929 square meters (approximately 0.093). To convert the other way, multiply square meters by 10.764 to get square feet.

## Question 21

A property has a built-up area of 1,200 sq ft. What is the approximate area in square meters?

- [ ] 100 sq m
- [x] 111 sq m
- [ ] 130 sq m
- [ ] 12,912 sq m

**Explanation:** 1,200 sq ft × 0.0929 = 111.48 sq m (approximately 111 sq m). This conversion is essential for working with international clients familiar with metric measurements.

---

## Question 22

Under Dubai law, what is the commission typically paid to the broker for a sales transaction?

- [ ] 1% from seller
- [x] 2% from seller (or as negotiated)
- [ ] 5% split between parties
- [ ] No fixed rate, purely negotiable

**Explanation:** The standard commission is 2% of the transaction value, typically paid by the seller. However, commission rates can be negotiated and should be clearly documented in the agency agreement.

---

## Question 23

A tenant has been paying rent consistently but the landlord wants to sell the property. What are the tenant's rights?

- [ ] Immediate eviction
- [ ] 30 days to vacate
- [x] The tenancy continues with the new owner under existing terms
- [ ] Automatic contract termination

**Explanation:** The sale of a property does not terminate an existing tenancy. The new owner assumes the landlord's obligations, and the tenant's rights continue under the existing lease terms until natural expiration.

## Question 24

What is the maximum penalty for advertising a property without a valid Trakheesi permit?

- [ ] AED 5,000
- [ ] AED 25,000
- [x] AED 50,000
- [ ] AED 100,000

**Explanation:** Advertising without valid Trakheesi registration can result in fines up to AED 50,000. All property advertisements must display the permit number prominently.

## Question 25

The escrow law requires developers to deposit what percentage of the land value before releasing units for sale?

- [ ] 10%
- [x] 20%
- [ ] 50%
- [ ] 100%

**Explanation:** Developers must deposit at least 20% of the land value with the escrow account trustee before they can begin selling off-plan units. This requirement ensures developers have skin in the game.

## Question 26

Which document confirms that a developer has no objection to the transfer of property ownership?

- [ ] Form F
- [ ] Oqood
- [x] No Objection Certificate (NOC)
- [ ] Title Deed

**Explanation:** The NOC (No Objection Certificate) is issued by the developer confirming all payments are cleared and there are no outstanding issues preventing the transfer of ownership.

## Question 27

A client asks about the cooling period for off-plan purchases. According to RERA regulations, buyers have:

- [x] No automatic cooling-off period (depends on developer policy)
- [ ] 7 days to cancel without penalty
- [ ] 14 days to cancel without penalty
- [ ] 30 days to cancel without penalty

**Explanation:** Unlike some jurisdictions, Dubai does not mandate a standard cooling-off period for property purchases. Any cancellation terms are governed by the individual sales contract with the developer.

## Question 28

What is the RERA regulation regarding handling client documents?

- [ ] Return all documents immediately after viewing
- [ ] Keep copies indefinitely
- [x] Maintain secure records and return originals promptly after use
- [ ] Destroy documents after transaction completion

**Explanation:** Agents must handle client documents securely, maintain proper records for the required retention period, and return original documents to clients promptly after they are no longer needed for the transaction.

## Question 29

Calculate the annual service charge for a 1,500 sq ft apartment with a service charge rate of AED 15 per sq ft:

- [ ] AED 15,000
- [x] AED 22,500
- [ ] AED 30,000
- [ ] AED 1,500

**Explanation:** Annual service charge = 1,500 sq ft × AED 15/sq ft = AED 22,500. Service charges cover building maintenance, security, common area upkeep, and other shared facilities.

## Question 30

Under RERA regulations, dual agency is:

- [ ] Prohibited in all circumstances
- [ ] Allowed without any disclosure
- [x] Permitted only with written disclosure and consent from both parties
- [ ] Required for all transactions

**Explanation:** Dual agency (representing both buyer and seller) is permitted in Dubai but requires full written disclosure to both parties and their informed consent before proceeding.

## Question 31

A property valued at AED 2,500,000 is sold. What is the total DLD registration fee?

- [ ] AED 50,000
- [ ] AED 75,000
- [x] AED 100,000
- [ ] AED 125,000

**Explanation:** DLD fee = AED 2,500,000 × 4% = AED 100,000. This fee is paid at the time of property transfer and registration with the Dubai Land Department.

## Question 32

What action should a broker take if they discover their client is attempting to use the transaction for money laundering?

- [ ] Complete the transaction and report afterward
- [ ] Warn the client and withdraw from the transaction
- [x] Refuse to proceed and report to the relevant authorities
- [ ] Continue if the funds appear legitimate

**Explanation:** Brokers must refuse to participate in any transaction suspected of money laundering and report their suspicions to the relevant authorities (FIU). Knowingly participating in money laundering is a criminal offense.

- [ ] 60 days before contract renewal

## Question 35

In property management, which of the following is typically NOT included in the service charge?

- [ ] Building security
- [ ] Common area maintenance
- [x] Individual unit repairs
- [ ] Swimming pool maintenance

**Explanation:** Service charges cover common areas and shared facilities. Individual unit repairs and maintenance inside the apartment are the owner's responsibility, not covered by the service charge.

## Question 36

A client wants to purchase a property for AED 2,000,000 to obtain the Golden Visa. What must they be aware of?

- [ ] The property must be residential only
- [ ] The property can have any mortgage amount
- [x] The property must be paid in full (no outstanding mortgage) for visa qualification
- [ ] Commercial properties qualify equally

## Question 37

What is the retention period for broker transaction records under RERA regulations?

- [ ] 1 year
- [ ] 3 years
- [x] 5 years
- [ ] 10 years

**Explanation:** Brokers must maintain transaction records, contracts, and related documentation for a minimum of 5 years. This includes all correspondence, contracts, and financial records.

## Question 38

A property generates AED 80,000 annual rent with annual expenses of AED 12,000. If the purchase price was AED 1,200,000, what is the net rental yield?

- [ ] 5.00%
- [x] 5.67%
- [ ] 6.67%
- [ ] 7.67%

**Explanation:** Net yield = (Annual Rent - Expenses) / Purchase Price × 100 = (AED 80,000 - AED 12,000) / AED 1,200,000 × 100 = AED 68,000 / AED 1,200,000 × 100 = 5.67%.

## Question 39

Under what circumstances can a landlord enter a rented property without tenant permission?

- [ ] Anytime during business hours
- [ ] With 24 hours notice for any reason
- [x] Emergency situations only, or with reasonable notice for inspections
- [ ] Never under any circumstances

**Explanation:** Landlords may only enter without permission in genuine emergencies (fire, flooding, etc.). For routine inspections or repairs, reasonable prior notice must be given and tenant consent obtained.

## Question 40

Which of the following represents proper ethical conduct for a real estate broker?

- [ ] Prioritizing transaction completion over client needs
- [ ] Sharing client information with other brokers to facilitate sales
- [ ] Accepting gifts from developers for promoting their projects
- [x] Disclosing all material facts and maintaining client confidentiality

**Explanation:** Ethical conduct requires full disclosure of material facts, maintaining client confidentiality, avoiding conflicts of interest, and always acting in the client's best interest regardless of personal gain.
