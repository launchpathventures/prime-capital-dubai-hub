# Client Communication Standards

Every message you send represents Prime Capital. This module establishes the communication standards that maintain our "antidote to the Dubai hustle" positioning.

## The Communication Principle

> **Quality over quantity. Relevance over frequency.**

Our clients are busy professionals. They don't want more messages â€” they want better ones. Every communication should pass a simple test: *Does this deserve their attention?*

## Response Time Standards

### The 2-Hour Rule (Active Clients)

For clients in an active transaction or those who have completed within the last 90 days:

**Standard:** Respond to every message within 2 business hours.

This doesn't mean a complete answer in 2 hours â€” it means acknowledgment and setting expectations:

> "Got your message about the payment schedule question. I'm checking with the developer's finance team now and will have clarity for you by end of day."

**Why 2 hours?** Your active clients are comparing you to other agents. They've likely contacted multiple people. The consultant who responds first, with substance, wins the relationship.

### The 24-Hour Rule (All Clients)

For all clients â€” active, past, or prospective:

**Standard:** Acknowledge every communication within 24 hours, even if you can't provide a complete answer yet.

**Example acknowledgment:**
> "Thank you for your message. I'm gathering the information you've requested and will have a full response for you by [specific day]. Please let me know if you need anything more urgently."

**Why this matters:** Fast acknowledgment shows you're attentive. Promising a specific timeline shows you're organised. Both build trust.

### When to Respond Immediately

Some messages warrant same-hour response:

- **Time-sensitive opportunities** â€” A property they expressed interest in has an update
- **Transaction milestones** â€” Completion dates, payment deadlines, document requirements
- **Direct questions with simple answers** â€” Don't make them wait for basic information
- **Expressions of concern** â€” If a client seems worried, address it promptly
- **Complaints or issues** â€” Never let a complaint sit unanswered

### When Not to Rush

Conversely, don't sacrifice quality for speed:

- **Complex analysis requests** â€” Take the time to be thorough
- **Sensitive topics** â€” Think carefully before responding
- **After-hours messages** â€” Unless genuinely urgent, respond the next business day

## The Prime Capital Voice in Writing

### What Our Clients Expect

Our clients are sophisticated. They recognise quality in communication just as they recognise quality in property. They expect:

- **Clarity** â€” No jargon, no ambiguity
- **Brevity** â€” Get to the point
- **Substance** â€” Real information, not filler
- **Professionalism** â€” Proper grammar, careful formatting

### Email Structure

Use this structure for most client emails:

1. **Opening line** â€” Acknowledge context or previous conversation
2. **Core message** â€” The main information, clearly stated
3. **Next steps** â€” What happens now, who does what
4. **Closing** â€” Professional sign-off with availability

**Example:**

> Good afternoon [Name],
>
> Following our conversation about Emaar Beachfront, I've confirmed availability for Unit 1502 in the tower we discussed.
>
> Key details:
> - 3-bedroom, 2,100 sq ft
> - Price: AED 4.2M
> - Completion: Q4 2027
> - Payment plan: 60/40
>
> I've attached the floor plan and specification sheet. The developer is holding three units at this price point, so this availability window is likely 7-10 days.
>
> Would you like to schedule a call to discuss further? I'm available tomorrow afternoon or Thursday morning.
>
> Best regards,
> [Your name]

### Language to Use

| Approach | Example |
|----------|---------|
| Direct | "The completion date has been confirmed as March 2027." |
| Specific | "The price includes a 15% completion discount until February 28." |
| Confident | "I recommend reviewing the upper floors for better views." |
| Helpful | "I'll prepare a comparison of both options for your review." |

### Language to Avoid

| Avoid | Better Alternative |
|-------|-------------------|
| "I just wanted to check in..." | "Following up on our conversation about..." |
| "Hope this email finds you well" | Get straight to the purpose |
| "Please don't hesitate to reach out" | "Let me know if you have questions" |
| "Best in class" / "World-leading" | Be specific about what makes it notable |
| Exclamation marks | Period. Professional tone. |

## Choosing Communication Channels

### Email

**Best for:**
- Detailed information that clients may need to reference later
- Attachments (documents, floor plans, contracts)
- Formal communications that need a paper trail
- Non-urgent updates

**Structure tip:** Use formatting (bullet points, headers) for longer emails. Make them scannable.

### Phone / Video Call

**Best for:**
- Complex discussions requiring back-and-forth
- Sensitive topics (pricing negotiations, concerns)
- Building rapport with new clients
- Urgent matters

**Protocol:** Always confirm the call with a brief agenda. Follow up with a summary email capturing key points and next steps.

### WhatsApp / Messaging

**Best for:**
- Quick, time-sensitive updates
- Confirming appointments
- Sharing quick photos or videos
- Clients who prefer this channel

**Caution:** WhatsApp can feel informal. Maintain professional tone. Don't send long messages â€” if it needs more than a paragraph, use email.

### In-Person Meetings

**Best for:**
- Property viewings
- Contract signing
- Relationship-building moments
- High-stakes discussions

**Protocol:** Always follow up with a written summary of what was discussed and agreed.

## Cultural Considerations by Nationality

Dubai's investor base is truly international. Adapting your communication style shows respect and builds trust faster.

### British Clients

- **Communication style:** Formal, understated, values politeness
- **What works:** Reserved confidence, avoiding overselling, self-deprecating humour
- **What doesn't:** Aggressive selling, excessive enthusiasm, pushing for quick decisions
- **Tip:** "I'd recommend..." works better than "You should..."

### Indian Clients

- **Communication style:** Relationship-first, family-oriented, values personal connection
- **What works:** Building rapport before business, asking about family, patience with decision-making
- **What doesn't:** Rushing the relationship, ignoring family dynamics
- **Tip:** Decisions often involve multiple family members â€” accommodate this

### Chinese Clients

- **Communication style:** Indirect, face-conscious, respects hierarchy
- **What works:** Patience, avoiding public disagreement, written confirmation of everything
- **What doesn't:** Direct confrontation, putting them on the spot
- **Tip:** "Let me check on that" is better than "No" â€” give face-saving options

### Russian Clients

- **Communication style:** Direct, values expertise, appreciates straightforwardness
- **What works:** Demonstrating knowledge, clear facts and figures, direct recommendations
- **What doesn't:** Vagueness, excessive small talk before business
- **Tip:** They appreciate confident expertise â€” be ready to back it up with data

### Middle Eastern Clients (GCC)

- **Communication style:** Relationship-based, hospitable, values trust over contracts
- **What works:** Taking time for pleasantries, flexibility, building personal trust
- **What doesn't:** Rushing to business, rigid processes
- **Tip:** Coffee/tea before business is expected â€” never decline hospitality

### European Clients (Continental)

- **Communication style:** Varies by country, generally formal, values professionalism
- **What works:** Punctuality, detailed documentation, process clarity
- **What doesn't:** Informal first-name basis too early, unstructured meetings
- **Tip:** Germans and Swiss particularly value precise information and timelines

### General Principles

- **Research before meetings** â€” Understand basic cultural norms
- **When in doubt, be more formal** â€” It's easier to relax than recover from offense
- **Follow their lead** â€” Let them set the communication tone
- **Never assume** â€” Ask about preferences rather than stereotyping

## Professional Boundaries

### Availability Expectations

Set clear expectations about your availability:

- **Standard business hours:** Clients can expect same-day response (2-hour for active)
- **Evenings and weekends:** You'll respond next business day unless urgent
- **Holiday periods:** Set out-of-office with alternative contact
- **Religious holidays:** Be aware of Ramadan, Diwali, Chinese New Year â€” acknowledge them

Communicate these expectations early in the relationship. Most clients respect boundaries when they're clearly stated.

### Personal vs. Professional

Build rapport, but maintain appropriate distance:

- **Do:** Remember personal details (family, interests, upcoming travel)
- **Do:** Acknowledge significant events (congratulations on a new role)
- **Don't:** Over-share personal information
- **Don't:** Make the relationship feel transactional by only reaching out for business
- **Do:** Send festival greetings appropriate to their culture

### Handling Difficult Conversations

Sometimes you'll need to deliver unwelcome news â€” a delayed completion, a price increase, a document issue.

**Approach:**
1. **Be direct** â€” Don't bury bad news in paragraphs of preamble
2. **Be factual** â€” Stick to what you know, not speculation
3. **Be solution-oriented** â€” What can be done now?
4. **Be available** â€” Offer to discuss by phone if they prefer

**Example â€” Delivering Bad News:**
> "I need to share an update on the completion timeline. [Developer] has notified us of a three-month delay to the Marina Gate project, with the revised completion now scheduled for September 2027.
>
> I know this isn't the news you were hoping for. I've reviewed the contract terms, and there are no financial implications for you from this delay. The developer has also offered [compensation/incentive] for affected buyers.
>
> I'm available to discuss this by phone whenever suits you. Would tomorrow morning work?"

## Putting It Into Practice

### Daily Habits

- Review your inbox at consistent times (morning, after lunch, end of day)
- Flag messages requiring more time, and schedule that time
- Before sending, re-read from the client's perspective

### Weekly Review

- Check for any clients you haven't been in touch with recently
- Review any outstanding follow-ups
- Update your CRM with recent interactions

### Quality Check

Before sending any client communication, ask:

- [ ] Does this deserve their attention?
- [ ] Is it clear what I'm communicating?
- [ ] Is there a specific next step or call to action?
- [ ] Does the tone match Prime Capital's voice?
- [ ] Have I proofread for errors?
