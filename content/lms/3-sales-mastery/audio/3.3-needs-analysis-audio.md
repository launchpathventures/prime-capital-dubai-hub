===
MODULE 3.3: Needs Analysis Conversation
TYPE: skills-script
===

### INTRO (60-90 seconds)

The needs analysis conversation determines everything that follows. Get this right, and you'll recommend the perfect property with confidence. Rush it, and you'll waste time showing unsuitable options.

Before presenting any property, you need to understand six key categories: motivation — why Dubai, why now, investment versus end-use. Budget — total capital, financing comfort, payment flexibility. Timeline — decision urgency, completion preferences. Criteria — location priorities, size requirements, must-haves versus nice-to-haves. Experience — first-time buyer or seasoned investor. And decision process — are they the sole decision-maker or is someone else involved?

Off-plan sales require deeper discovery because the buyer can't see the finished product. Payment plan structure affects affordability, completion timeline impacts planning, and ROI expectations need grounding in reality.

This module gives you the structured framework for discovery conversations. You'll learn to use the adapted SPIN model: Situation, Problem, Implication, and Need-Payoff questions specifically for real estate.

Here's the goal: ten minutes of discovery saves hours of wrong showings. The best salespeople listen seventy percent and talk only thirty percent.

Let's learn to ask better questions.

---

### DEMO (3-5 minutes)

**SETUP**

Let me show you two versions of a discovery conversation with a prospective investor from the UK who has enquired about Dubai property. Watch for how the questioning flows and what information each agent gathers.

**WEAK EXAMPLE**

*[Scene: Agent jumps straight into questions without framing]*

Agent: "So, what's your budget?"

Investor: "Um, I'm not sure yet. Depends on what's available."

Agent: "Okay. Where do you want to buy?"

Investor: "I've heard Dubai Marina is good."

Agent: "Right. How many bedrooms?"

Investor: "Maybe one or two."

Agent: "Great, I'll send you some options. When can you come for viewings?"

Investor: "I... I haven't really thought about that. I'm just exploring at this stage."

Agent: "Okay, let me know when you're ready."

*[Conversation ends with no clear next step]*

**COACH ANALYSIS — What Went Wrong**

Three major problems. First, interrogation mode — rapid-fire questions with no context make the investor feel like they're filling out a form, not having a conversation. Second, surface-level answers — the agent accepted "I'm not sure" and "depends" without probing deeper. Third, no understanding of motivation — we have no idea WHY this person is looking at Dubai property. Are they chasing yield? Do they want a Golden Visa? Are they relocating? Without that context, any property recommendation is a guess.

**STRONG EXAMPLE**

*[Scene: Agent frames the discovery conversation properly]*

Agent: "Great to connect, James. Before I start sharing options, I'd love to understand a bit more about what you're looking for. That way, I can make sure I'm showing you properties that actually fit your goals. Should take about ten minutes. Sound good?"

Investor: "Sure, makes sense."

Agent: "Perfect. So, what's sparked your interest in Dubai property right now?"

Investor: "I've been looking at international property for a while. The yields in the UK are terrible, and I've heard Dubai offers much better returns."

Agent: "That's a common driver for UK investors. What kind of yield are you targeting? And are you thinking you'd manage this remotely, or would you prefer a fully managed solution?"

Investor: "Ideally eight to ten percent net. And definitely fully managed — I don't want the hassle."

Agent: "Got it. And in terms of budget, what range are you thinking for this purchase?"

Investor: "Probably around a million dirhams, maybe one point two."

Agent: "That opens up good options. Are you planning to pay cash or would you look at financing for any portion?"

Investor: "Cash is available, but I'd consider leverage if it makes sense."

Agent: "Understood. And timeline — are you looking to make a decision in the next few weeks, or is this more of a research phase?"

Investor: "If I find the right thing, I could move quickly. I'd say within the next month."

Agent: "Perfect. Let me make sure I've understood correctly. You're looking at Dubai primarily as a yield investment, budget around one to one point two million, prefer fully managed, cash available but open to leverage, and you could decide within a month if we find the right fit. Does that capture it?"

Investor: "Yes, that's exactly it."

Agent: "Excellent. Based on what you've shared, I have two or three developments in mind that fit really well. I can send you the details to review, and then we can schedule a call to walk through them together. How does that sound?"

**COACH ANALYSIS — What Made It Work**

First, proper framing — the agent positioned discovery as being in the investor's interest, not just a box-ticking exercise. Second, open questions — "What sparked your interest" invites a real answer, not a yes or no. Third, follow-up depth — when the investor mentioned yields, the agent drilled into specific expectations and management preferences. Fourth, the summary confirmation — repeating back what you heard ensures you're aligned before moving forward. And fifth, a clear next step — the conversation ended with a concrete action.

**KEY DIFFERENCE**

The single biggest difference? The strong example understood the "why" behind the purchase. The weak example collected facts without context. Knowing someone wants "a one-bedroom in Marina" is useless if you don't know they're motivated by rental yield and want hands-off management. The "why" shapes every recommendation that follows.
