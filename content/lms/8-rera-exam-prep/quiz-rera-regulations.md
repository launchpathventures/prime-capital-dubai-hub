# RERA Laws & Regulations Quiz

## Question 1

Which law established RERA as the regulatory authority for Dubai's real estate sector?

- [ ] Law No. 7 of 2006 (Land Registration Law)
- [x] Law No. 16 of 2007
- [ ] Law No. 26 of 2007 (Landlord-Tenant Law)
- [ ] Law No. 13 of 2008 (Interim Property Registration)

**Explanation:** Law No. 16 of 2007 established the Real Estate Regulatory Agency (RERA) as the regulatory arm of Dubai Land Department, responsible for licensing and market regulation.

---

## Question 2

Under the Landlord-Tenant Law (Law No. 26 of 2007), what notice period must a landlord provide for eviction due to personal use?

- [ ] 30 days
- [ ] 90 days
- [x] 12 months via notary public
- [ ] 6 months

**Explanation:** For personal use eviction, landlords must provide 12 months written notice through notary public or registered mail. The notice must state the reason and be served before the contract renewal date.

---

## Question 3

What is the registration deadline for tenancy contracts under Ejari?

- [ ] 7 days from signing
- [ ] 14 days from signing
- [x] 30 days from signing
- [ ] 60 days from signing

**Explanation:** All tenancy contracts must be registered in the Ejari system within 30 days of signing. Failure to register can affect legal enforceability and access to government services.

---

## Question 4

What is the maximum penalty for advertising property without a valid Trakheesi permit?

- [ ] AED 10,000
- [ ] AED 25,000
- [x] AED 50,000
- [ ] AED 100,000

**Explanation:** Advertising without valid Trakheesi registration can result in fines up to AED 50,000. This applies to all advertising channels including online, print, and social media.

---

## Question 5

According to Dubai law, which percentage correctly represents the DLD property registration fee?

- [ ] 2%
- [x] 4%
- [ ] 5%
- [ ] 10%

**Explanation:** The DLD registration fee is 4% of the property value for all transactions. This fee is typically split between buyer and seller (2% each) unless otherwise negotiated.

---

## Question 6

Law No. 13 of 2008 governs:

- [ ] Broker licensing requirements
- [x] Interim registration of off-plan properties (Oqood)
- [ ] Owners Association establishment
- [ ] Rental dispute resolution

**Explanation:** Law No. 13 of 2008 established the interim property registration system (Oqood) for off-plan properties, protecting buyer interests before project completion.

---

## Question 7

What is the statutory notice period for a landlord to request a rent increase?

- [ ] 30 days before renewal
- [ ] 60 days before renewal
- [x] 90 days before renewal
- [ ] 120 days before renewal

**Explanation:** Landlords must provide at least 90 days written notice before the contract renewal date if they wish to increase rent. The increase must comply with RERA Rental Index guidelines.

---

## Question 8

Under the RERA Rental Index, what is the maximum rent increase permitted when current rent is 11-20% below market average?

- [ ] 5%
- [x] 10%
- [ ] 15%
- [ ] 20%

**Explanation:** The RERA Rental Index uses a tiered system: no increase (0-10% below average), 5% (11-20% below), 10% (21-30% below), 15% (31-40% below), 20% (over 40% below).

---

## Question 9

What is the penalty for operating as a real estate broker without a valid RERA license?

- [ ] AED 50,000 fine
- [x] Fines up to AED 1 million and potential criminal prosecution
- [ ] Warning letter only
- [ ] Mandatory training course

**Explanation:** Unlicensed practice is a serious violation. Penalties can include fines up to AED 1 million, criminal prosecution, and permanent prohibition from the industry.

---

## Question 10

Which law governs the establishment and operation of Owners Associations in Dubai?

- [ ] Law No. 16 of 2007
- [ ] Law No. 26 of 2007
- [x] Jointly Owned Property Law (Strata Law)
- [ ] Law No. 13 of 2008

**Explanation:** The Jointly Owned Property Law (Strata Law) establishes the framework for Owners Associations, governing common property management, voting rights, and dispute resolution.

---

## Question 11

How long must brokers retain transaction records under RERA regulations?

- [ ] 1 year
- [ ] 3 years
- [x] 5 years
- [ ] 10 years

**Explanation:** Brokers must maintain all transaction records, contracts, correspondence, and financial documents for a minimum of 5 years from the date of transaction.

---

## Question 12

What is the time limit for filing a complaint with RERA regarding a licensed broker?

- [ ] 6 months
- [x] 1 year
- [ ] 2 years
- [ ] No time limit

**Explanation:** Complaints against licensed brokers must generally be filed within one year of the incident. Early filing with supporting documentation is recommended.

---

## Question 13

Under the escrow law, what is the minimum percentage of land value a developer must deposit before selling off-plan units?

- [ ] 10%
- [x] 20%
- [ ] 50%
- [ ] 100%

**Explanation:** Developers must deposit at least 20% of the land value with the escrow trustee before they are permitted to sell off-plan units. This protects buyers from speculative projects.

---

## Question 14

What is the maximum security deposit permitted by law for an unfurnished residential property?

- [ ] One month's rent
- [x] 5% of annual rent
- [ ] 10% of annual rent
- [ ] Three months' rent

**Explanation:** For unfurnished properties, the maximum security deposit is 5% of annual rent. For furnished properties, the maximum is 10% of annual rent.

---

## Question 15

Which body has jurisdiction over landlord-tenant disputes in Dubai?

- [ ] Civil Court
- [ ] RERA directly
- [x] Rental Dispute Center (RDC)
- [ ] Dubai Police

**Explanation:** The Rental Dispute Center (RDC) is the specialized tribunal with exclusive jurisdiction over landlord-tenant disputes, including evictions, rent disputes, and contract interpretation.
