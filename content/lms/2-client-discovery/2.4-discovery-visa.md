# Discovery: Visa-Seekers

## Learning Objective

By the end of this module, you will be able to **qualify Golden Visa seekers efficiently** using a discovery script that uncovers their real motivation for residency, family composition, budget constraints, timeline pressures, and understanding of requirementsâ€”while correcting common misconceptions.

---

## Why Visa-Seeker Discovery Matters

Visa-seekers think they want a property. What they actually want is what the visa enables: residency, banking access, business formation, family security, or a Plan B.

The property is a means to an end. Your job is to understand the endâ€”then recommend the right means.

Visa-seeker discovery must uncover:
- **Primary motivation:** Why do they want UAE residency?
- **Family composition:** Who will be included on the visa?
- **Understanding level:** Do they know the actual requirements?
- **Budget reality:** Can they actually qualify (AED 2M, paid off)?
- **Timeline pressure:** Is there urgency (visa expiring, business need)?
- **Secondary interests:** Do they also want yield, lifestyle, or appreciation?

Many visa-seekers have misconceptions. Part of discovery is gentle education.

---

## Common Misconceptions to Correct

Before diving into the script, understand what visa-seekers often get wrong:

| Misconception | Reality |
|---------------|---------|
| "I can use a mortgage" | Property must be fully paid off for visa purposes |
| "Any property works" | Must be residential (not commercial) |
| "AED 2M total" | AED 2M is the minimum; can be multiple properties |
| "Immediate visa" | Process takes 3-6 weeks after title deed |
| "Visa is forever" | 10-year visa, renewableâ€”but must maintain property ownership |
| "I have to live in Dubai" | No minimum stay requirement |

Correct these gently in conversation. Don't lecture; clarify when misconceptions appear.

## The Visa-Seeker Discovery Script

### Opening: Understand Their Starting Point

<speak>

<speak>

*Why this works: You've uncovered two motivations (business and security) and explored whether it's about living in Dubai or just having the option.*

**Key follow-ups:**

- "Is banking access in the UAE important for you?"
- "Are there business formation needs driving this?"

<speak>

**Consultant:** "Beyond the visa, what else matters to you about this property? Would you live in it, rent it out, or keep it empty as a holiday base?"

- "If this property appreciated significantly, would you consider selling itâ€”even though that would affect your visa?"
- "How important is the property's quality? Some visa-buyers don't care about the actual asset; others want something they'd be proud to own."
- "Would you ever use this as a holiday home?"

## Common Mistakes

**1. Assuming the visa is the only goal**
Many visa-seekers also want yield, appreciation, or personal use. Explore secondary interestsâ€”they shape property selection.

**2. Not confirming cash availability**
"I have the budget" doesn't mean they have liquid cash. Confirm they can actually pay without a mortgage.

**3. Overwhelming with requirements**
Don't list every rule and exception. Clarify what they need to know, and address details as they become relevant.

**4. Ignoring the family dimension**
Who's on the visa matters. Parents require separate applications. Children over certain ages have different rules. Map the full family situation.
