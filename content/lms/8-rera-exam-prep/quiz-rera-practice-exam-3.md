# RERA Practice Exam 3

## Exam Instructions

- **Time Limit:** 60 minutes
- **Questions:** 40 multiple choice
- **Passing Score:** 75% (30/40 correct)
- **Format:** Select the single best answer for each question

Read each question carefully. Manage your time—spend no more than 90 seconds per question.

---

## Question 1

Which document serves as the initial agreement between a buyer and seller before the final transfer?

- [x] Form F (Memorandum of Understanding)
- [ ] Title Deed
- [ ] Affection Plan
- [ ] Trakheesi Permit

**Explanation:** Form F is the standard MOU used in Dubai real estate transactions. It documents the agreed terms, deposit, and conditions before the parties proceed to final transfer at DLD.

---

## Question 2

A broker license in Dubai is valid for:

- [x] 1 year from date of issue
- [ ] 2 years from date of issue
- [ ] 5 years from date of issue
- [ ] Indefinitely once obtained

**Explanation:** RERA broker licenses must be renewed annually. Continuing education requirements must also be met for license renewal.

---

## Question 3

According to RERA, client confidentiality extends to:

- [ ] Only financial information
- [ ] Only personal contact details
- [x] All information obtained during the professional relationship
- [ ] Information specifically marked confidential

**Explanation:** Confidentiality obligations cover all information obtained during the professional relationship, including personal, financial, and transactional details, unless disclosure is legally required.

---

## Question 4

A landlord can request rent increase only when:

- [ ] The tenant agrees verbally
- [ ] At any time with notice
- [x] At lease renewal, with 90 days notice, within RERA index limits
- [ ] Immediately after major renovations

**Explanation:** Rent increases can only be requested at contract renewal, must comply with the RERA Rental Index caps, and require 90 days advance written notice.

---

## Question 5

The escrow account for off-plan developments is managed by:

- [ ] The developer directly
- [ ] The buyer's bank
- [x] A RERA-approved trustee bank
- [ ] Dubai Municipality

**Explanation:** Off-plan escrow accounts must be held at RERA-approved trustee banks. Developers cannot directly access these funds; releases are linked to construction milestones.

---

## Question 6

What is the RERA procedure when a broker changes brokerage companies?

- [ ] License automatically transfers
- [ ] License becomes void
- [x] Must apply for license transfer through RERA
- [ ] Must obtain a new license

**Explanation:** Brokers must apply to RERA for license transfer when changing companies. The new employer must sponsor the broker, and continuity requirements must be met.

---

## Question 7

Which of the following statements about service charges is TRUE?

- [ ] Service charges are fixed by government regulation
- [ ] Service charges cannot exceed 5% of property value
- [x] Service charges are based on actual costs and approved budgets
- [ ] Service charges are optional for owners

**Explanation:** Service charges reflect actual operational costs and are determined by the Owners Association based on approved annual budgets. They are mandatory for all unit owners.

---

## Question 8

A buyer discovers a material defect 30 days after transfer that was not disclosed. They can:

- [ ] Automatically void the transaction
- [ ] Demand immediate refund
- [x] Pursue legal remedies for misrepresentation if the defect was known and concealed
- [ ] File criminal charges against the broker

**Explanation:** If a material defect was known and deliberately concealed, the buyer may have legal recourse for misrepresentation. The remedy depends on the specifics and may include damages or rescission.

---

## Question 9

What document confirms that a property is free of any encumbrances?

- [ ] Form F
- [ ] Ejari registration
- [x] Title Deed and NOC showing no outstanding amounts
- [ ] Trakheesi permit

**Explanation:** A clear Title Deed combined with NOC from the developer confirms no liens, mortgages, or outstanding service charges. Buyers should verify both before proceeding.

---

## Question 10

Under RERA regulations, what must be included in all property advertisements?

- [ ] Broker's personal phone number
- [x] Trakheesi permit number
- [ ] Developer contact information
- [ ] Price per square foot

**Explanation:** All property advertisements must display a valid Trakheesi permit number. This applies across all media including online portals, print, and social media.

---

## Question 11

Calculate the gross rental yield for a property purchased at AED 850,000 with annual rent of AED 55,000:

- [ ] 5.5%
- [ ] 6.0%
- [x] 6.47%
- [ ] 7.0%

**Explanation:** Gross yield = (AED 55,000 / AED 850,000) × 100 = 6.47%. This represents the annual return before deducting any expenses.

---

## Question 12

The Real Estate Institute (REI) is responsible for:

- [ ] Issuing title deeds
- [x] Providing professional education and training for real estate practitioners
- [ ] Setting property prices
- [ ] Collecting property taxes

**Explanation:** REI provides the educational programs and professional development courses required for RERA licensing and continuing education.

---

## Question 13

A tenant sublets their apartment without landlord permission. The landlord can:

- [ ] Immediately evict without notice
- [x] Serve notice and seek eviction through RDC
- [ ] Only increase the rent
- [ ] Take no action until lease renewal

**Explanation:** Unauthorized subletting is a lease violation that can grounds for eviction. However, the landlord must follow proper legal process through the Rental Dispute Center.

---

## Question 14

What is the primary purpose of the Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements?

- [ ] To verify property specifications
- [ ] To assess client's budget
- [x] To prevent money laundering and verify client identity
- [ ] To qualify for Golden Visa

**Explanation:** KYC requirements are designed to verify client identity, understand the source of funds, and prevent money laundering and terrorist financing in real estate transactions.

---

## Question 15

A villa has a plot area of 5,000 sq ft and a built-up area of 3,500 sq ft. The FAR (Floor Area Ratio) is:

- [ ] 1.43
- [x] 0.7
- [ ] 70%
- [ ] 35%

**Explanation:** FAR = Built-up Area / Plot Area = 3,500 / 5,000 = 0.7. This ratio indicates how much of the plot has been developed versus remaining open space.

---

## Question 16

Under the Strata Law, voting rights in the Owners Association are based on:

- [ ] One vote per owner
- [x] Percentage of ownership in common areas
- [ ] Length of ownership
- [ ] Property value

**Explanation:** Voting rights are proportional to each owner's share in the common areas, typically based on the relative size of their unit compared to total development area.

---

## Question 17

A broker learns that a property they're selling has been involved in a civil lawsuit. They must:

- [ ] Wait for the buyer to ask about legal issues
- [ ] Only disclose if the lawsuit affects the title
- [x] Disclose any known material legal issues to potential buyers
- [ ] Refer the matter to a lawyer only

**Explanation:** Any known legal issues that could materially affect the property or transaction must be disclosed to potential buyers. This is a fundamental ethical obligation.

---

## Question 18

The standard security deposit for a furnished residential rental is:

- [ ] One month's rent
- [ ] 5% of annual rent
- [x] 10% of annual rent
- [ ] Three months' rent

**Explanation:** For furnished properties, landlords can collect a security deposit up to 10% of annual rent. For unfurnished properties, the maximum is 5%.

---

## Question 19

What is the consequence of operating as a real estate broker without a valid license?

- [ ] Warning letter only
- [x] Fines, potential criminal charges, and prohibition from the industry
- [ ] Required to pay back-dated license fees
- [ ] No consequence if transactions are successful

**Explanation:** Unlicensed practice is a serious violation that can result in substantial fines (up to AED 1 million), criminal prosecution, and permanent ban from the industry.

---

## Question 20

The RERA Smart Hub system is used for:

- [ ] Social media marketing
- [x] Unified platform for real estate services and transactions
- [ ] Developer project approvals only
- [ ] Mortgage applications

**Explanation:** RERA Smart Hub (formerly DLD Smart systems) provides a unified digital platform for real estate services including transactions, complaints, and professional services.

---

## Question 21

Convert 2,000 square feet to square meters:

- [ ] 18.58 sq m
- [ ] 150 sq m
- [x] 185.8 sq m
- [ ] 21,528 sq m

**Explanation:** 2,000 sq ft × 0.0929 = 185.8 sq m. This conversion is essential for working with international clients and comparing properties globally.

---

## Question 22

A developer offers a broker a 1% bonus for every unit sold above target. The broker should:

- [ ] Accept without disclosure
- [ ] Reject the offer entirely
- [x] Disclose the incentive to their clients
- [ ] Keep it confidential from the brokerage

**Explanation:** Any incentives that could influence recommendations must be disclosed to clients. This ensures transparency and allows clients to make informed decisions.

---

## Question 23

What is the landlord's obligation regarding property maintenance?

- [ ] Tenant is responsible for all maintenance
- [x] Landlord is responsible for structural maintenance and major systems
- [ ] Maintenance responsibility is always 50/50
- [ ] Municipality handles all maintenance

**Explanation:** Landlords are responsible for maintaining the property's structure, major systems (HVAC, plumbing, electrical), and ensuring the property remains habitable. Day-to-day minor maintenance may fall to the tenant.

---

## Question 24

A property's annual rent is AED 120,000 with annual service charges of AED 18,000. Net rental income is:

- [ ] AED 138,000
- [x] AED 102,000
- [ ] AED 120,000
- [ ] AED 18,000

**Explanation:** Net rental income = Gross Rent - Expenses = AED 120,000 - AED 18,000 = AED 102,000. Service charges are deducted as they represent an ongoing expense.

---

## Question 25

The Rental Dispute Center's decisions can be appealed to:

- [ ] RERA directly
- [ ] Dubai Police
- [x] Dubai Court of Appeals
- [ ] There is no appeal process

**Explanation:** RDC decisions can be appealed to the Dubai Court of Appeals within the specified appeal period (typically 15 days from the ruling).

---

## Question 26

Under what circumstance can a tenant claim priority to purchase their rented property?

- [ ] After 3 years of tenancy
- [ ] When the landlord decides to sell
- [x] Only if such right is explicitly granted in the tenancy contract
- [ ] Tenants always have first right of refusal

**Explanation:** There is no automatic right of first refusal for tenants. Such rights only exist if explicitly agreed upon in the tenancy contract or a separate agreement.

## Question 27

What is the purpose of a Power of Attorney (POA) in real estate transactions?

- [ ] To transfer ownership permanently
- [x] To authorize another person to act on behalf of the principal
- [ ] To register a complaint with RERA
- [ ] To certify property valuations

**Explanation:** A POA legally authorizes another person to act on behalf of the principal in specified matters, including signing documents and completing transactions.

## Question 28

Calculate the DLD registration fee for a property purchased at AED 4,500,000:

- [ ] AED 90,000
- [ ] AED 135,000
- [x] AED 180,000
- [ ] AED 225,000

**Explanation:** DLD fee = AED 4,500,000 × 4% = AED 180,000. This is typically split between buyer and seller (2% each) unless otherwise negotiated.

---

## Question 29

A broker's fiduciary duty includes all of the following EXCEPT:

- [ ] Loyalty to the client
- [ ] Disclosure of material facts
- [x] Guaranteeing a specific sale price
- [ ] Reasonable care and skill

**Explanation:** Brokers cannot guarantee outcomes like sale price. Fiduciary duties include loyalty, disclosure, confidentiality, obedience to lawful instructions, and reasonable care—not guaranteed results.

---

## Question 30

What is the procedure for a landlord to evict a tenant for non-payment of rent?

- [ ] Change locks immediately
- [ ] File police report
- [x] Provide notice and file case with Rental Dispute Center
- [ ] Withhold security deposit and demand vacation

**Explanation:** Even for non-payment, landlords must follow legal process: provide written notice, allow time for the tenant to cure the breach, and if unresolved, file for eviction through the RDC.

---

## Question 31

Which of the following is TRUE about exclusive listing agreements?

- [ ] The broker can refuse to show the property to all buyers
- [x] The owner agrees to work only with the designated broker for a specified period
- [ ] The owner cannot sell the property themselves
- [ ] Multiple brokers can hold exclusive listings simultaneously

**Explanation:** Exclusive listings grant the designated broker sole rights to market the property for a specified period. The owner typically can still sell directly but may owe commission.

---

## Question 32

A property management company must provide owners with financial reports:

- [ ] Only when requested
- [x] At regular intervals as required by the Owners Association
- [ ] Once every 5 years
- [ ] Never, as this is confidential

**Explanation:** Property management companies must provide regular financial reports to the Owners Association, typically quarterly and annually, showing income, expenses, and reserve fund status.

The RERA code of ethics requires brokers to:

- [ ] Accept all clients regardless of circumstances
- [ ] Prioritize high-value transactions
- [x] Treat all parties honestly and fairly
- [ ] Work exclusively with developer projects

**Explanation:** The code requires honest and fair treatment of all parties, even those the broker doesn't directly represent. This maintains market integrity and professional standards.

What percentage of common areas is typically allocated for parking in residential developments?

- [x] Varies by development—determined by master plan and municipality regulations

## Question 35

A tenant's security deposit should be returned within:

- [ ] 7 days of vacating
- [x] A reasonable period after inspection (typically within 30 days)
- [ ] 90 days
- [ ] 1 year

**Explanation:** Security deposits should be returned within a reasonable period after move-out inspection, typically within 30 days. Deductions for damages must be documented and justified.

## Question 36

What is the role of a property valuer in Dubai?

- [ ] To set selling prices
- [x] To provide independent assessment of property market value
- [ ] To approve mortgage applications
- [ ] To certify building safety

**Explanation:** Property valuers provide independent, professional assessments of market value based on comparable sales, property condition, and market analysis. They must be RERA licensed.

## Question 37

A property with annual rent of AED 90,000 and purchase price of AED 1,100,000. Calculate the gross yield:

- [ ] 7.0%
- [ ] 7.5%
- [x] 8.18%
- [ ] 9.0%

## Question 38

Under RERA regulations, a broker must disclose:

- [ ] Only information specifically requested by the client
- [ ] Only positive information about the property
- [x] All material facts that could affect the client's decision
- [ ] Information that benefits the transaction

**Explanation:** Material facts are any information that could reasonably affect a party's decision. Brokers must disclose all such facts regardless of whether they help or hinder the transaction.

---

## Question 39

What is the consequence of false representation in a real estate transaction?

- [ ] Warning only for first offense
- [x] License suspension/revocation, fines, and potential civil/criminal liability
- [ ] Requirement to complete additional training
- [ ] No consequence if the buyer doesn't complain

**Explanation:** False representation is a serious violation that can result in license action, financial penalties, and legal liability including civil damages and criminal prosecution.

## Question 40

A property manager fails to maintain adequate building insurance. Who bears the liability?

- [ ] Individual unit owners
- [x] The property management company and potentially the Owners Association board
- [ ] The developer
- [ ] No liability exists

**Explanation:** Property managers have a duty to maintain adequate insurance. Failure to do so exposes them and the OA board to liability for any uninsured losses.
