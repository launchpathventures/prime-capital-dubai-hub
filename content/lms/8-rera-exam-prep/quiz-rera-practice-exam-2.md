# RERA Practice Exam 2

## Exam Instructions

- **Time Limit:** 60 minutes
- **Questions:** 40 multiple choice
- **Passing Score:** 75% (30/40 correct)
- **Format:** Select the single best answer for each question

Read each question carefully. Manage your time—spend no more than 90 seconds per question.

---

## Question 1

What is the minimum education requirement to obtain a RERA broker license in Dubai?

- [ ] High school diploma
- [x] Completion of RERA-certified training course
- [ ] University degree in real estate
- [ ] No formal education required

**Explanation:** All brokers must complete a RERA-certified training course and pass the broker examination. While there is no specific academic degree requirement, the certification course is mandatory.

---

## Question 2

When must a real estate agent disclose their agency relationship to a potential buyer?

- [x] At the earliest opportunity before providing services
- [ ] Only when asked by the client
- [ ] At the time of contract signing
- [ ] Disclosure is not required

**Explanation:** Agency disclosure must occur at the earliest opportunity, typically at first contact. This allows the potential client to make an informed decision about engaging the agent's services.

---

## Question 3

According to RERA regulations, what is the maximum commission rate for rental transactions?

- [ ] One month's rent
- [ ] One week's rent
- [x] 5% of annual rent (or as negotiated)
- [ ] 2% of annual rent

**Explanation:** The standard rental commission is 5% of the annual rent value, though this is negotiable between parties. For commercial properties, rates may differ based on market practice.

---

## Question 4

A landlord wishes to increase rent by 15% at renewal. The current rent is 25% below the RERA Index average. Is this increase permitted?

- [ ] No, increases are capped at 5%
- [x] Yes, increases up to 15% are permitted when rent is 21-30% below market
- [ ] Yes, increases up to 20% are always permitted
- [ ] No, rent cannot be increased during a contract term

**Explanation:** The RERA Rental Index allows tiered increases based on how far below market the current rent is. For rents 21-30% below average, increases up to 15% are permitted.

---

## Question 5

What is the purpose of the Ejari system in Dubai?

- [ ] To calculate property taxes
- [x] To register all tenancy contracts with the government
- [ ] To track property ownership transfers
- [ ] To manage broker licenses

**Explanation:** Ejari is the mandatory tenancy contract registration system. All rental agreements must be registered within 30 days of signing to be legally enforceable and for tenants to access government services.

---

## Question 6

An off-plan buyer defaults on their payment plan. Under RERA regulations, the developer must:

- [ ] Immediately cancel the contract and retain all payments
- [ ] Refund 100% of payments made
- [x] Follow the termination provisions in the SPA with proper notice
- [ ] Transfer the unit to another buyer

**Explanation:** Developers must follow the contractual termination provisions and provide proper notice. The buyer's rights depend on the completion percentage of the project and specific contract terms.

---

## Question 7

What document is required to change a property's registered use from residential to commercial?

- [ ] New title deed
- [ ] Form F
- [x] Approval from relevant authorities and updated Affection Plan
- [ ] NOC from developer only

**Explanation:** Changing property use requires approval from Dubai Municipality and DLD, along with an updated Affection Plan reflecting the new permitted use. This is a significant regulatory process.

## Question 8

The DLD administrative fee for property registration is:

- [ ] AED 250
- [x] AED 580
- [ ] AED 1,000
- [ ] AED 2,000

**Explanation:** In addition to the 4% registration fee, DLD charges a fixed administrative fee of AED 580 for processing the property transfer documentation.

---

## Question 9

A property management company can charge owners for which of the following without specific approval?

- [ ] Major renovations
- [ ] Legal fees for disputes
- [x] Routine maintenance within approved budget
- [ ] Capital improvements

**Explanation:** Property management companies can perform and charge for routine maintenance that falls within the pre-approved annual budget. Major expenditures and capital improvements require owner/OA approval.

---

## Question 10

According to RERA ethics, which practice is prohibited?

- [ ] Advertising properties on social media
- [ ] Working with multiple clients simultaneously
- [x] Making false or misleading statements about property features
- [ ] Charging commission for services rendered

**Explanation:** Making false or misleading statements about any aspect of a property—including size, condition, legal status, or potential returns—is strictly prohibited and can result in license revocation.

## Question 11

A buyer agrees to purchase a property for AED 1,500,000 with a 10% deposit. How much is the deposit?

- [ ] AED 100,000
- [x] AED 150,000
- [ ] AED 175,000
- [ ] AED 200,000

**Explanation:** Deposit = AED 1,500,000 × 10% = AED 150,000. The deposit is typically held by the broker or developer until transaction completion or release to the seller.

## Question 12

What is the legal status of verbal agreements for property sales in Dubai?

- [ ] Fully enforceable if witnessed
- [ ] Valid for transactions under AED 500,000
- [x] Not legally enforceable—written contracts required
- [ ] Enforceable for 30 days only

**Explanation:** Property transactions in Dubai must be documented in writing to be legally enforceable. Verbal agreements have no legal standing in real estate transactions.

## Question 13

Under what circumstances can a tenant withhold rent payment?

- [ ] When the landlord refuses repairs
- [ ] When disputing service charges
- [x] Only with a court order or RDC ruling
- [ ] When rent increase is requested

**Explanation:** Tenants cannot unilaterally withhold rent. Any disputes must be resolved through the Rental Dispute Center (RDC), and rent should continue to be paid until a ruling is issued.

## Question 14

A broker discovers another agent is working with their exclusive client. What is the appropriate action?

- [ ] Immediately terminate the relationship with the client
- [ ] Report the other agent to the police
- [x] Document the situation and address with the client and brokerage management
- [ ] Interfere with the other agent's showing

**Explanation:** The broker should document the situation, review the exclusivity terms with the client, and if necessary, address through proper channels including brokerage management and potentially RERA.

## Question 15

What is the "Right of First Refusal" in Dubai real estate?

- [ ] A tenant's right to purchase the property
- [x] A contractual right giving one party priority to match any offer received
- [ ] A developer's right to buy back units
- [ ] A government right to acquire property

**Explanation:** Right of First Refusal is a contractual provision that gives one party (often an existing tenant or partner) the right to match any bona fide offer before the property is sold to a third party.

## Question 16

Which law governs landlord-tenant relationships in Dubai?

- [ ] Law No. 16 of 2007
- [x] Law No. 26 of 2007 (as amended by Law No. 33 of 2008)
- [ ] Law No. 7 of 2006
- [ ] Federal Law No. 5 of 2007

**Explanation:** Law No. 26 of 2007, amended by Law No. 33 of 2008, governs all landlord-tenant relationships in Dubai, establishing rights, obligations, and dispute resolution procedures.

---

## Question 17

A developer fails to deliver a project on time. What is the buyer's remedy under RERA regulations?

- [ ] Automatic contract cancellation
- [ ] Immediate full refund
- [x] Refer to SPA terms and file complaint with RERA/RDC if terms not honored
- [ ] No remedy available

**Explanation:** Buyer remedies depend on the SPA terms regarding delay. If the developer fails to honor contractual provisions, buyers can file complaints with RERA or pursue claims through the Rental Dispute Center.

## Question 18

To convert 100 square meters to square feet, you multiply by:

- [ ] 0.0929
- [x] 10.764
- [ ] 3.281
- [ ] 1.196

**Explanation:** 1 square meter = 10.764 square feet. Therefore, 100 sq m × 10.764 = 1,076.4 square feet. This is the inverse of the sq ft to sq m conversion.

## Question 19

What is the purpose of an Affection Plan?

- [ ] To show the property's mortgage status
- [x] To show the property's official registered boundaries and specifications
- [ ] To display the property's rental history
- [ ] To list the property's owners

**Explanation:** The Affection Plan is an official document showing the property's exact boundaries, area, location within the building, and registered specifications. It is essential for transactions.

## Question 20

A real estate agent receives a gift worth AED 10,000 from a developer. According to RERA ethics, the agent should:

- [ ] Accept and keep the gift confidential
- [x] Disclose the gift to their client and employer
- [ ] Return the gift immediately
- [ ] Report the developer to RERA

**Explanation:** Any gifts or incentives received must be disclosed to both the client and employer to maintain transparency. Non-disclosure could constitute a conflict of interest violation.

## Question 21

What is the notice period for a landlord seeking eviction due to the property requiring major renovation?

- [ ] 90 days
- [x] 12 months
- [ ] 6 months
- [ ] 30 days

**Explanation:** For renovation eviction, landlords must provide 12 months notice via notary public. They must also demonstrate that the renovation cannot be done with the tenant in residence.

## Question 24

The Owners Association in Dubai is governed by:

- [ ] Federal Commercial Law
- [ ] RERA licensing regulations

## Question 25

A buyer pays AED 200,000 deposit on a Form F agreement but later decides not to proceed. What typically happens to the deposit?

- [x] Forfeited to the seller (unless otherwise agreed)
- [ ] Returned in full
- [ ] Split between buyer and seller
- [ ] Held by RERA indefinitely

**Explanation:** Under standard Form F terms, if the buyer defaults without valid cause, the deposit is typically forfeited to the seller as liquidated damages. Specific terms may vary by contract.

## Question 26

- [x] Permitted subject to developer approval and applicable fees

## Question 31

A property has a BUA (Built-Up Area) of 1,000 sq ft and a plot area of 1,500 sq ft. What percentage is the built-up area?

- [ ] 50%
- [ ] 60%
- [x] 66.7%
- [ ] 150%

**Explanation:** BUA percentage = (1,000 / 1,500) × 100 = 66.7%. This ratio is relevant for villas and townhouses where plot size differs from built-up area.

- [ ] 48 hours

## Question 36

What is the relationship between RERA and the Dubai Land Department (DLD)?

- [ ] They are competing agencies
- [ ] RERA regulates DLD
- [x] RERA is the regulatory arm of DLD
- [ ] They operate independently

**Explanation:** RERA operates as the regulatory and licensing arm of the Dubai Land Department. DLD handles registration and title matters while RERA oversees professional licensing and market regulation.

## Question 37

Calculate the total transaction cost for a buyer purchasing a AED 1,800,000 property (including 4% DLD fee and AED 580 admin fee):

- [ ] AED 72,000
- [x] AED 72,580
- [ ] AED 90,000
- [ ] AED 180,580

**Explanation:** DLD fee = AED 1,800,000 × 4% = AED 72,000. Admin fee = AED 580. Total = AED 72,000 + AED 580 = AED 72,580. This excludes other costs like agent commission.

## Question 38

Which of the following is considered unethical conduct for a real estate broker?

- [ ] Recommending a property inspection
- [ ] Suggesting the client seek legal advice
- [x] Providing estimated rental returns without proper market analysis
- [ ] Disclosing known property defects

**Explanation:** Making unfounded claims about returns without proper market analysis can mislead clients and constitutes a violation of professional ethics. All projections should be based on documented market data.

## Question 40

A broker receives two offers on an exclusive listing: one from their own buyer client at AED 2M, another from an outside agent's buyer at AED 2.1M. The ethical approach is to:

- [ ] Present only the higher offer
- [ ] Present only their own client's offer
- [x] Present both offers to the seller transparently
- [ ] Negotiate between buyers before presenting

**Explanation:** The broker has a fiduciary duty to the seller (listing client) to present all bona fide offers. The seller has the right to consider all options and make an informed decision.
