# Discovery: Investors

## Learning Objective

By the end of this module, you will be able to **qualify an investment buyer using a complete discovery script** that uncovers investment objectives, risk tolerance, experience level, portfolio context, decision timeline, and financing approachâ€”positioning you as a strategic advisor, not a property broker.

---

## Why Investor Discovery Is Different

Investors think in numbers. But the numbers that matter vary dramatically.

One investor wants the highest possible yieldâ€”doesn't care about capital growth, views property as an income stream. Another wants capital appreciationâ€”happy with 4% yield if the property doubles in value. A third wants neitherâ€”they want currency diversification and couldn't care less about the Dubai market.

Investor discovery must uncover:
- **Investment objective:** Yield, growth, diversification, or combination
- **Risk tolerance:** Conservative, moderate, or aggressive
- **Experience level:** First-time investor or seasoned portfolio holder
- **Portfolio context:** How Dubai fits with their existing investments
- **Decision process:** Sole decision or involving advisors/family
- **Timeline and urgency:** Active buyer or long-term explorer
- **Budget and financing:** Cash, mortgage, or structured

Get these wrong, and you'll show yield-focused properties to a growth investor, or off-plan to someone who wants immediate income.

## The Investor Discovery Script (Complete)

### Opening: Position Yourself as Advisor

<speak>
**Consultant:** "Before we discuss specific properties, I'd like to understand your investment goals and approach. That way I can focus on opportunities that actually align with what you're trying to achieve. I'll ask some questions about your objectives, timeline, and experienceâ€”is that okay?"

**Client:** "Sure, go ahead."

**Consultant:** "Perfect. Let's start with the big picture."
</speak>

*Why this works: You've positioned the conversation as strategic, not transactional. You've asked permission, creating engagement.*

### Section 1: Investment Objective

<speak>
**Consultant:** "When you think about property investment in Dubai, what's your primary goal? Are you focused on rental income, capital growth, or something else like diversification or residency?"

**Client:** "Mainly rental income. I want passive cash flow."

**Consultant:** "Got itâ€”yield is the priority. Is capital appreciation a secondary benefit, or are you purely income-focused and would even consider something that might not appreciate much?"
</speak>

*Why this works: You've identified the primary driver and explored whether secondary goals exist.*

**Key follow-ups for yield-focused:**
- "What yield range are you targeting? What would make this investment worthwhile for you?"
- "Are you comfortable with higher-yielding areas that might have more volatility, or do you prefer established locations even if yields are lower?"
- "How important is tenant quality versus absolute yield?"

**Key follow-ups for growth-focused:**
- "What's your investment horizon? When would you expect to potentially exit?"
- "Are you comfortable with off-plan and the timing risk, or do you prefer ready properties?"
- "How do you think about liquidityâ€”ability to sell when you need to?"

**Key follow-ups for diversification:**
- "What's driving your interest in Dubai specifically versus other markets?"
- "Is currency exposure part of your strategyâ€”AED is pegged to USD?"
- "How does this fit with your existing portfolio allocation?"

### Section 2: Experience Level

<speak>
**Consultant:** "Have you invested in property beforeâ€”either in Dubai or elsewhere?"

**Client:** "Yes, I own two apartments in London and one in Mumbai."

**Consultant:** "Great, so you understand the process. What's your experience been like with those? Any lessons you've learned that shape what you're looking for now?"
</speak>

*Why this works: Experience shapes expectations. A seasoned investor needs less education; a first-timer needs more guidance.*

**Key follow-ups:**
- "Have you invested in off-plan before? How did that go?"
- "Do you manage your properties yourself or use property management?"
- "Have you had any experiencesâ€”good or badâ€”that influence what you're looking for?"

### Section 3: Portfolio Context

<speak>
**Consultant:** "You mentioned properties in London and Mumbai. How does Dubai fit into your overall investment strategy?"

**Client:** "I want to diversify away from pound exposure. And Mumbai is family property, not really an investment."

**Consultant:** "So Dubai is partly currency diversificationâ€”the dirham is pegged to the dollar, which gives you USD exposure. Is that part of the appeal?"

**Client:** "Exactly."
</speak>

*Why this works: You've uncovered a motivation that wasn't immediately obviousâ€”currency hedging.*

**Key follow-ups:**
- "Are there other asset classes you're considering for this capital, or is property the clear choice?"
- "How does this investment rank in terms of your priorities right now?"
- "Are you looking at other markets besides Dubai?"

### Section 4: Budget & Financing

<speak>
**Consultant:** "Let's talk about budget. What investment size are you comfortable with?"

**Client:** "I'm thinking somewhere around three million dirhams."

**Consultant:** "That opens up a good range of options. Would you be paying cash, or would you want to leverage with a mortgage?"

**Client:** "Cash. I want to avoid debt."

**Consultant:** "Understood. Is there flexibility in that budget if we found an exceptional opportunityâ€”say, at three-fiveâ€”or is three million a ceiling?"
</speak>

*Why this works: You've established budget, financing preference, and flexibility without being pushy.*

**Key follow-ups if using mortgage:**
- "Are you a UAE resident, or would this be a non-resident mortgage?"
- "Have you explored lending options here? Rates are different from UK/EU."
- "What loan-to-value ratio are you comfortable with?"

**If budget is unclear or vague:**
<speak>
**Consultant:** "I want to make sure I'm showing you relevant options. Some investors are looking at one to two million for entry-level yield, others at five million plus for premium assets. Where do you see yourself on that spectrum?"
</speak>

### Section 5: Timeline & Decision Process

<speak>
**Consultant:** "What's your timeline looking like? Are you actively ready to invest, or still in research mode?"

**Client:** "I'm ready. I want to deploy this capital in the next three months."

**Consultant:** "Good to know. When it comes to making the decision, is that something you'll decide on your own, or are there others involvedâ€”spouse, business partner, financial advisor?"

**Client:** "Just me. I'll run it past my accountant for tax implications, but the decision is mine."
</speak>

*Why this works: You've established urgency and decision structure.*

**Key follow-ups:**
- "Have you looked at other properties already? Where are you in the process?"
- "What would need to happen for you to feel confident making a decision?"
- "Are there any dates or deadlines driving thisâ€”tax year, capital availability?"

### Section 6: Risk Tolerance & Concerns

<speak>
**Consultant:** "Every investment has trade-offs. What concerns, if any, do you have about investing in Dubai property?"

**Client:** "Honestly, I've heard about developers delaying projects. That worries me."

**Consultant:** "That's a fair concernâ€”developer delays have happened. Does that mean you'd prefer ready properties, or are you open to off-plan if the developer has a strong track record?"

**Client:** "I'd consider off-plan if I knew the developer was reliable."
</speak>

*Why this works: You've surfaced a concern and immediately addressed it by exploring their tolerance for risk mitigation.*

**Key follow-ups:**
- "Are there other risks you're thinking aboutâ€”market volatility, currency, tenant issues?"
- "How do you typically approach risk in your investmentsâ€”conservative, moderate, aggressive?"
- "What would make you walk away from an opportunity?"

### Section 7: The Goal Behind the Goal

<speak>
**Consultant:** "One more question. You mentioned rental income is the priority. Can I askâ€”what does that rental income mean for you? Is it supplementing your income, building toward retirement, or something else?"

**Client:** "Honestly? My job is demanding, and I'm not getting younger. I'm building passive income so I have options in five to ten years."

**Consultant:** "So this is about creating freedomâ€”financial independence over time."

**Client:** "Exactly."
</speak>

*Why this works: You've uncovered the real motivation. It's not about yield; it's about freedom. This changes how you frame recommendations.*

### Closing: Confirm and Propose Next Steps

<speak>
**Consultant:** "Let me summarize to make sure I've got this right. You're looking to invest around three million dirhams, cash, primarily for rental yield. You want solid, reliable income to build toward financial independence in five to ten years. You'd consider off-plan if the developer is reputable, but your main concern is delivery risk. **Client:** "That's spot on."

**Consultant:** "Based on what you've told me, I think we should look at a few different angles. </speak>

*Why this works: You've confirmed understanding, proposed a strategic approach, and secured commitment to next steps.*

## The "Goal Behind the Goal" Technique

This is one of the most powerful questions in discovery:

<speak>
**Consultant:** "Can I askâ€”what does [stated goal] actually mean for you? Why is that important?"
</speak>

**Examples:**

| Stated Goal | Goal Behind the Goal |
|-------------|---------------------|
| "I want high yield" | "I'm building passive income for early retirement" |
| "I want capital growth" | "I'm protecting wealth against inflation in my country" |
| "I want a Dubai property" | "My business needs a UAE presence for banking" |
| "I want something safe" | "I lost money in crypto and want tangible assets" |

The stated goal tells you what. The goal behind the goal tells you why. The why shapes your recommendation.

## Common Mistakes

**1. Accepting "good investment" as an answer**
When a client says "I want a good investment," push further. Good for what? Income, growth, safety, tax efficiency? "Good" means nothing without context.

**2. Quoting yield without knowing their baseline**
"Six percent yield" means nothing if they expect ten percent. Establish their target before presenting numbers.

**3. Skipping the decision process question**
Many investors have advisors, partners, or family who influence decisions. Discover this early to avoid surprises later.
