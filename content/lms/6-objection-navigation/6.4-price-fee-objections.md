## Introduction

"Isn't that expensive for Dubai?" "What about hidden fees?" "Can you get me a better price?"

Price and fee objections are among the most predictable—and most mishandled—objections in real estate. Many consultants either become defensive or immediately offer discounts, neither of which builds trust or closes transactions.

This module teaches you to address pricing concerns with transparent cost breakdowns, comparative analysis, and value-based positioning that transforms price resistance into confident purchase decisions.

## Common Price & Fee Objections

### The Four Categories

1. **Absolute Price** — "That's too expensive"
2. **Relative Price** — "I can get similar property cheaper elsewhere"
3. **Hidden Costs** — "What fees am I not being told about?"
4. **Negotiation** — "Can you get me a discount?"

Each requires different evidence and approach.

## Coach Walkthrough: The Hidden Fees Concern

<speak>
This is one of the most important objections to handle well. International investors have often been burned by unexpected costs in other markets—closing costs that doubled the budget, surprise taxes, ongoing fees they didn't know about. Your ability to provide a complete, transparent cost breakdown builds enormous trust.
</speak>

**Investor:** "I've read articles about hidden fees in Dubai real estate. What costs am I not being told about? I don't want surprises six months from now."

**Consultant:** "That's exactly the right question to ask—and I appreciate you asking it directly. Unexpected costs destroy budgets and trust. Let me show you every single cost involved in purchasing this property, and I mean every single one.

I'm going to walk you through the complete breakdown so there are zero surprises."

<speak>
I've validated the concern and committed to complete transparency. This immediately differentiates me from agents who gloss over costs or provide vague estimates. Now I'm going to show a detailed spreadsheet.
</speak>

[Shares screen: Complete cost breakdown]

**Consultant:** "Here's the property: AED 2,400,000 purchase price. Let me show you every cost from today until you have keys in hand.

**Purchase Costs:**

| Cost Item | Amount | When Due | Notes |
|-----------|---------|----------|-------|
| Property Price | AED 2,400,000 | Per payment plan | The unit price |
| DLD Transfer Fee | AED 96,000 (4%) | At transfer | Government registration fee |
| DLD Trustee Fee | AED 4,000 (fixed) | At transfer | Standard processing |
| Real Estate Agent Fee | AED 48,000 (2%) | At transfer | Agency commission (paid by buyer in secondary; varies for off-plan) |
| Mortgage Arrangement (if financing) | AED 12,000-24,000 | At mortgage closing | Bank fees if using financing |
| Property Valuation (if financing) | AED 2,500-3,500 | Before mortgage | Required by banks |
| **Total Purchase Costs** | **AED 162,500-177,500** | - | **~6.8-7.4% of price** |

**Annual Ownership Costs:**

| Cost Item | Amount | Frequency | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-----------|-------|
| Service Charge | AED 18,000-22,000 | Annual | Covers common area maintenance |
| Chiller Fees (if applicable) | AED 8,000-12,000 | Annual | District cooling charges |
| DEWA (utilities) | AED 3,000-6,000 | Annual | Water and electricity if vacant |
| Property Management (if renting out) | AED 5% of annual rent | Annual | Optional but recommended |
| **Total Annual Costs** | **AED 29,000-40,000** | - | **~AED 2,500-3,500/month** |

That's it. Those are ALL the costs. There are no:
- Capital gains taxes when you sell (zero)
- Annual property taxes (zero)
- Inheritance taxes (zero)
- Stamp duty beyond the 4% DLD fee (zero)

The 4% DLD fee is your only government tax on this transaction. Everything else is either service charges for maintenance or optional financing costs if you choose to mortgage."

**Investor:** "Okay, that's clearer than I expected. But AED 160,000+ in transaction costs is significant—can any of that be negotiated?"

<speak>
Good—the investor is now engaged in the details, not worried about hidden costs. Now I can address what's negotiable and what's not.
</speak>

**Consultant:** "Let me tell you exactly what's fixed and what has flexibility:

**Fixed (zero negotiation):**
- DLD Transfer Fee (4%) — Government fee, non-negotiable
- DLD Trustee Fee (AED 4,000) — Government fee, fixed
- Service charges — Set by developer based on actual costs
- DEWA / chiller — Utility companies, fixed rates

**Sometimes Negotiable:**
- Agent commission — In secondary market, sometimes sellers cover this. For off-plan, commission structure varies by developer.
- Property price — Depends on market, developer, and inventory levels. I'll tell you honestly if there's negotiation room.

For this specific property, the developer is offering a limited-time promotion: free DLD transfer fee (4% savings = AED 96,000) if you sign the SPA before month-end. That's a real cost saving that reduces your total purchase costs from AED 162,500 to AED 66,500.

That promotion is why I recommended viewing this project this week—the offer ends in 12 days. But I want to be clear: this is a legitimate developer promotion, not a pressure tactic. If you need more time to decide, that's fine—we can look at other options without the time pressure."

<speak>
I've shown complete transparency, separated fixed from negotiable costs, and mentioned a time-sensitive promotion without being pushy about it. The investor now has all the information to make an informed decision.
</speak>

## The Relative Price Objection

### "I can get a similar property cheaper in [other area]"

<details>
<summary>Full Response Script</summary>

**Acknowledge:** "Absolutely—price comparison is smart due diligence. Let me show you what you're actually comparing."

**Provide Analysis:**

"You mentioned a property in [other area] at AED 1,800/sq ft vs this property at AED 2,100/sq ft. Let's compare what you're actually getting:

[Shows comparison table]

| Factor | This Property (Downtown) | Comparison (JVC) |
|--------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| Price/sq ft | AED 2,100 | AED 1,800 |
| Location | Dubai Mall 5 min walk | 20 min drive to Metro |
| Rental Yield | 5.5% | 7.2% |
| Rental Rate | AED 110K/year | AED 85K/year |
| Appreciation (5yr) | 45% avg | 22% avg |
| Resale Liquidity | High (30-45 days) | Moderate (60-90 days) |

So yes, JVC is cheaper per square foot—but you're comparing a prime downtown location with premium amenities to a suburban community. They're different products serving different investor goals.

The question is: what's your priority?

**If yield:** JVC at 7.2% rental yield beats Downtown at 5.5%. You'd earn AED 85K/year vs AED 110K, but on AED 700K less investment—better cash-on-cash return.

**If capital growth:** Downtown historically appreciates faster. Over 5 years, you'd likely see AED 600K+ capital growth here vs AED 250K in JVC.

**If resale liquidity:** Downtown properties sell in 30-45 days typically. JVC takes 60-90 days. If you need to exit quickly, location matters.

There's no 'right' answer—just which factors matter most for your investment goals. What's your priority: yield, growth, or liquidity?"

</details>

### "That seems expensive for Dubai—I thought property was cheap here"

<details>
<summary>Full Response Script</summary>

**Acknowledge:** "Dubai property is generally affordable compared to London, New York, or Singapore—but 'cheap' depends on the location and property type. Let me show you the context."

**Provide Comparison:**

"This property is AED 2,400,000 (approximately £540,000 / $650,000).

Let's compare to similar properties in other global cities:

| City | Similar 2BR Apartment | Price | Yield |
|------|----------------------|-------|-------|
| Dubai (Marina) | 1,100 sq ft, waterfront | £540,000 | 6.5% |
| London (Canary Wharf) | 750 sq ft, riverside | £650,000 | 3.8% |
| New York (Jersey City) | 900 sq ft, waterfront | $750,000 | 4.2% |
| Singapore (Marina Bay) | 850 sq ft, central | £720,000 | 3.1% |

Dubai offers:
- More space for the price (1,100 sq ft vs 750-900 sq ft)
- Higher rental yield (6.5% vs 3-4%)
- Zero property tax (vs 1-2% annual in UK/US)
- Golden Visa eligibility at £445K ($545K) threshold

So Dubai is competitive on price, superior on yield and space, and has zero ongoing property taxes.

Within Dubai, this property is mid-market. Budget properties start at AED 800K in areas like International City. Premium properties in Palm Jumeirah reach AED 10M+. This is priced in the 'premium accessible' range—quality location and amenities, but not ultra-luxury pricing."

</details>

## The Negotiation Objection

### "Can you get me a 10% discount?"

<details>
<summary>Full Response Script</summary>

**Acknowledge:** "I appreciate you asking directly. Let me tell you exactly what's possible and what's not with this property."

**Provide Honest Assessment:**

"Here's the reality: this is a new off-plan launch from Emaar. They're selling at fixed prices for the first 60 days of launch. After that, pricing adjusts based on sales pace—if they sell 50%+ in 60 days, prices go up 5-8%. If they sell slower, there may be flexibility.

Right now, with 78 units sold out of 420 in the first 3 weeks, they're tracking to the faster scenario. That means zero discount availability—in fact, prices will likely increase next month.

What IS available:
- Current launch price: AED 2,400,000
- Free DLD transfer fee (4% = AED 96,000 savings) if you sign SPA this month
- Flexible payment plan: 20% down, 80% over 3 years during construction

So instead of a price discount, you're getting AED 96,000 in real cost savings plus favorable payment terms.

If this property doesn't work at this price, I can show you alternatives where there IS negotiation room—typically resale properties from motivated sellers or projects that haven't sold as quickly. But I won't waste your time claiming I can negotiate where I can't. Would you like to see what else is available?"

</details>

### Wrong: Immediately Offering to Negotiate

**❌ "Let me see what I can do—I'll ask the developer for a discount."**

**Why it fails:** If you immediately offer to negotiate without assessing, the client assumes you were overpricing initially. You've lost credibility.

### Right: Honest Assessment First

**✅ "Let me tell you exactly what's negotiable here and what's not. [Provides analysis]. If there's no flexibility here, I'd rather show you alternatives where we do have negotiation leverage."**

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Wrong: Hiding Costs Until Later

Only disclosing service charges after SPA is signed, surprising client with AED 25K annual cost.

**Why it fails:** Destroys trust completely. Creates legal and reputational risk.

### ✅ Right: Full Transparency Upfront

Show complete cost breakdown in first serious meeting, before any commitment.

---

### ❌ Wrong: Comparing to Incomparable Properties

"This Downtown property is cheaper per sq ft than Palm Jumeirah"

**Why it fails:** Different locations, different value propositions. Apples to oranges.

### ✅ Right: Like-for-Like Comparisons

"Here are three properties in Downtown, all 2-bedroom, delivered 2024-2026. Let me show you price per sq ft and what each offers..."

---

### ❌ Wrong: Promising Discounts You Can't Deliver

"I'll get you 10% off—let me talk to the developer"
[Then returns with 2% or nothing]

**Why it fails:** Overpromised and underdelivered. Client now distrusts everything else you say.

### ✅ Right: Setting Realistic Expectations

"On new launches, developers rarely discount in the first 60 days. If negotiation is a priority, I'd recommend focusing on resale properties where sellers have more flexibility."

## Why Price Objections Matter

Handling price and fee objections effectively enables you to:

1. **Build trust through transparency** about all costs, not just purchase price
2. **Differentiate from competitors** who hide costs or make unrealistic promises
3. **Convert value-conscious investors** who need full financial clarity
4. **Reduce post-sale disputes** by setting accurate cost expectations upfront
5. **Command premium positioning** by demonstrating value, not competing on price

The investors who ask the hardest questions about price are often the most serious buyers—they're doing financial due diligence, not price shopping. Your transparency converts them.

## Key Takeaways

- Price objections are mostly Understanding Objections requiring transparency and comparison
- Total transaction costs in Dubai: 6-7% (mainly 4% DLD fee)—show complete breakdown upfront
- Annual ownership costs: AED 30-40K for typical apartments (service charges + utilities)
- No property tax, no capital gains tax, no inheritance tax—major advantage vs other markets
- Compare like-for-like when addressing relative price objections (same location tier, completion date)
- Never promise discounts you can't deliver—honesty about negotiation limits builds more trust
