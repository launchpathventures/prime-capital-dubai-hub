# First Contact Excellence

## Learning Objective

By the end of this module, you will be able to **respond to a new lead within 60 seconds** using a script that opens dialogue, establishes credibility, and secures a next step‚Äîall without sounding like a salesperson.

---

## Why Response Time Changes Everything

When someone enquires about a property, they're interested *right now*. Five minutes later, they've messaged three other agents. Ten minutes later, they're back to browsing. An hour later, they can't remember which agent seemed competent.

**The data:**

| Response Time | Result |
|---------------|--------|
| Under 5 minutes | 21x more likely to qualify |
| 5-30 minutes | Competitive disadvantage begins |
| Over 1 hour | They've spoken to 2-3 competitors |
| Next day | Essentially cold outreach |

The 60-second window isn't about being pushy‚Äîit's about being helpful when they actually want help.

## The Prime Capital First Contact Script

This is your primary script. Master this before learning variations.

**Scenario:** A lead enquired about an off-plan project through Property Finder.

### The Script (Annotated)

<speak>
**You:** "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Prime Capital. You just enquired about [Project Name]‚ÄîI'm actually working with several clients on that development right now."
</speak>

*Why this works: You've confirmed you're responding to their enquiry (not cold calling), and you've established credibility without bragging. "Working with several clients" positions you as active and knowledgeable.*

<speak>
**You:** "Is now a good time for a quick two-minute conversation?"
</speak>

*Why this works: You're respecting their time and asking permission. Most agents launch into a pitch. You're already different.*

**If they say yes:**

<speak>
**You:** "Perfect. Before I share any details, I'd like to understand what you're looking for. Are you considering this for investment, or would you be living there yourself?"
</speak>

*Why this works: You've pivoted to understanding them immediately. You're an advisor seeking to help, not a salesperson seeking to sell.*

**If they're busy:**

<speak>
**You:** "No problem at all. When would work better for you‚Äîtomorrow morning or afternoon? I'll send you my direct WhatsApp with some information about the development in the meantime."
</speak>

*Why this works: You've proposed a specific alternative (not "I'll call back sometime") and you've created a reason to follow up via another channel.*

### What Makes This "Quiet Luxury"

Notice what the script **doesn't** do:

- No pressure ("This is selling fast! Limited units!")
- No pitch (price, features, amenities)
- No assumptions about what they want
- No desperation ("I'd love to help you!")
- No generic ("How can I help you today?")

The tone is confident, unhurried, professional. You're the advisor they're fortunate to have reached.

## Handling "I'm Busy Right Now"

This will happen often. Here's how to handle it gracefully:

<speak>
**Client:** "Actually, I'm in a meeting. Can you call back?"

**You:** "Of course. Would tomorrow morning or afternoon work better for you? I'll send you a quick WhatsApp with my details and some information on the project so you have it when you're ready."

**Client:** "Tomorrow afternoon is fine."

**You:** "Perfect‚ÄîI'll call around 3pm. And just so you know, I'm here if any questions come up before then. Talk soon."
</speak>

**Why this works:**
- You've locked in a specific time
- You've created a reason to follow up on WhatsApp
- You've positioned yourself as available without being needy
- You ended with confidence, not desperation

**1. Starting with a pitch**

‚ùå "Hi, I'm calling about DAMAC Lagoons‚Äîwe have amazing 3-bedroom villas starting from 1.5 million..."

‚úÖ Open with who you are, why you're calling, and a question.

**2. Sounding scripted or robotic**

‚ùå Reading word-for-word without natural flow

‚úÖ Know the structure, but adapt your tone to the person. If they're relaxed, be relaxed.

**3. Ending without a next step**

‚ùå "Great, well let me know if you have questions. My number is..."

‚úÖ Always propose something specific: a time to talk, information to send, or a meeting to schedule.

## Quick Reference Card

**Before you call:**
- Lead name (pronunciation if unusual)
- Property/project they enquired about
- Lead source (shapes your approach)
- Quiet environment, full attention

**The call structure:**
1. Greeting + context (10 sec)
2. Permission check (5 sec)
3. Opening question (10 sec)
4. Listen and qualify (varies)
5. Propose next step (10 sec)

**If busy:**
- Specific alternative time
- WhatsApp follow-up
- Leave them feeling valued, not interrupted

## Key Takeaways

- Respond within 5 minutes‚Äîspeed is your competitive advantage, but quality beats speed alone
- Open with questions, not pitches‚Äîyou're an advisor, not a telemarketer
- Always secure a specific next step‚Äîvague endings lose opportunities
- The script is a framework‚Äîadapt your tone to match the person
- "Quiet luxury" means confident, unhurried, and focused on them‚Äînot desperate or pushy

**You are calling:** Sarah Mitchell, 38, British expat currently living in Singapore. Works in finance, considering relocating to Dubai for work.

**Her situation (reveal gradually):**
- Budget: AED 1.5-2 million
- Goal: Apartment to live in initially, investment potential secondary
- Timeline: 4 weeks to decide, job starts in 3 months
- Concerns: Doesn't know Dubai areas, worried about choosing without seeing in person
- She's contacted 4 agencies‚Äîyou're one of them

**Her communication style:**
- Professional and direct
- Skeptical of sales tactics
- Appreciates efficiency and expertise
- Responds well to good questions
- Put off by pressure, small talk, or generic pitches

**Her enquiry:** 2BR apartment in Business Bay, AED 1.8M, sent 5 minutes ago via Property Finder.

**Your objectives:**
1. Open appropriately using the Prime Capital approach
2. Qualify her situation (timeline, experience, priorities)
3. Address her concern about buying remotely
4. Secure a next step (not a vague "I'll send you some listings")

**Feedback criteria:**
- Quality of opening (confident, clear, permission-seeking)
- Qualification questions asked (meaningful, not interrogation)
- How remote purchase concerns were handled
- Whether a specific next step was secured
- Overall tone: advisory vs salesy
- Rating: Needs Work / Competent / Strong
